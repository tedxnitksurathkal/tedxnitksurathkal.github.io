"Solve for y"

A simple phrase takes us all back to math class at school. The question has followed us all the way into engineering. Ever stopped to think about what the point is? What's the point of this abstract problem we're asked to solve? Maybe it's time we solved the more primary problem for whY.

What is true learning? In today's competitive world, the rat race for certain degrees and careers for the sake of status and better salary prospects is taking a toll on students, inducing more stress and insecurity. People's true passions take a backseat, and their creative interests are suppressed. Is the education system partially to blame for this? If so, what can be done to break out of this vicious cycle?

TEDxNITKSurathkal brings you the latest edition of our Salon event, titled Solve for y. Join us on the 4th of November as our speakers delve deep into the nature of learning; What ideal learning looks like which can impart knowledge and at the same time prepare one for the real world, and whether the current Indian education system, with all its recent add-ons like NEP, is aligned with this.
